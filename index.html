Efo riro

A plate of efo riro

Efo Riro is a rich vegetable soup that is native to the Yorubas of Western Nigeria.
The vegetables that can be used to cook this soup are Efo Shoko or Efo Tete (Green Amaranth). If these are not readily available where you live, leafy or frozen spinach is a very good substitute.the soup can been eaten with any swallow of your choice such as:amala,eba,fufu,rice and pounded yam.

Ingredients

S/N          Ingredients          Measurements
1           Green vegetables (efo tete)          3-4 bunches
2          Red bell peppers (tatashe)           3 Red bell peppers
3          Scotch bonnet chillie          3 scotch bonnet chillie
4          Onions          2 onions
5          Palm oil          1 cup palm oil
6          Locust beans          3 tbsp locust beans
7          Smoked mackerel          1 whole smoked mackerel
8          Smoked ponmo          2 medium Smoked ponmo
9          Ground crayfish          2 tbsp Ground crayfish
10          Salt          add to taste
11          Maggi          2 cubes

Steps/Methods

  1.    Place a pan on medium heat and add palm oil, heat for about 5 minutes but don't bleach
  2.    Add locust beans and chopped onions    and fry for about 2 minutes (In this case, I used half of the locust beans and saved the rest before I finished cooking the soup)
  3.    Add the blended pepper to the palm oil and stir to combine, after about 3 minutes, add the remaining locust beans. Add the diced ponmo and ground crayfish and cook for another 3-4 minutes on low heat
  4.    Add seasoning (bouillon cube/stock) and pinch of salt. (check for salt at the later stage of the cooking)
  5.    Whilst you are waiting for the sauce to cook, boil water enough to cover the vegetable. Once the water is boiled, pour over the vegetable and cover with a lid for 5 minutes. Strain and squeeze out excess water from the vegetable.
  6.    Continue to cook the sauce till oil floats on the top, add half of the smoked fish and stir to combine. (You can add little water to the sauce if it is too dry but do not over do it as efo riro is almost water free)
  7.    Continue to cook the sauce till oil floats on the top, add half of the smoked fish and stir to combine. (You can add little water to the sauce if it is too dry but do not over do it as efo riro is almost water free)
  8.    Add the remaining smoked mackerel and carefully combine in the vegetable. Check for salt and cook for another 4 minutes.
